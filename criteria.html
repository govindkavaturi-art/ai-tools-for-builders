Evaluation Criteria

How we score and rank AI tools. Transparent, signal-based, no pay-to-play.

The Formula

Every tool gets a combined score from 0-100 based on real activity signals and builder utility.

combined_score = (activity_score × 0.6) + (relevance_score × 0.4)

Tools need a combined score of 40 or higher to be listed. No exceptions.

Activity Score (60% weight)

We scan GitHub, HackerNews, Product Hunt, and X every day at 6 AM PST. Real signals, not marketing spend.

Positive Signals

Signal Points
Product Hunt featured launch (30 days) +50
GitHub trending +40
Hacker News front page +35
Product Hunt 500+ upvotes +30
New funding round announced +30
GitHub active commits (10+ in 30 days) +25
GitHub stars gained (30 days) up to +20
Google Trends rising interest +20
X mentions by builders (1K+ followers) +15/mention (max 45)

Decay Penalties

Condition Points
No signals for 30+ days -25
No signals for 60+ days -50
Confirmed shutdown -100

Relevance Score (40% weight)

How useful is this tool for builders shipping real products?

Factor Points
High-value category (coding, automation, agents, productivity) +25
Other categories +15
Working product (not waitlist) +20-25
Free tier available +20
Affordable pricing ($10-30/mo) +15
Hot/trending tags +10

Tool States

Tools move between states based on their combined score.

✅
Active
Score ≥ 40
⚠️
Watchlist
Score 25-39
☠️
Graveyard
Score < 25 for 14 days

Tools on the watchlist get a 14-day grace period. If they don't recover above 40, they're moved to the graveyard with a reason (INACTIVITY, SHUTDOWN, SUPERSEDED, etc).

Graveyard Reasons

Reason What It Means
INACTIVITY No signals for 60+ days
SHUTDOWN Company closed operations
ACQUIRED Acquired and deprecated
SUPERSEDED Better alternatives emerged
PIVOTED No longer AI-focused
VAPORWARE Never delivered on promises
No Pay-to-Play
